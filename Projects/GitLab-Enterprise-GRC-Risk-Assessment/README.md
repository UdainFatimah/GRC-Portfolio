# GitLab Enterprise GRC Risk Assessment

> **A public-information-based Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) assessment of GitLab, demonstrating enterprise cyber risk analysis, information security governance, and compliance mapping.**
---

## Overview

This project presents a structured Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) assessment of **GitLab**, a global DevSecOps platform. Using publicly available information, I evaluated GitLab's business environment, identified critical assets, assessed enterprise cyber risks, mapped key compliance requirements, and developed practical risk treatment recommendations.

The objective was not to perform penetration testing or identify technical vulnerabilities, but to apply GRC principles from a business and risk management perspective.

---

## Objectives

* Understand GitLab's business and operational environment
* Identify critical business and technical assets
* Assess and prioritize enterprise cyber risks
* Map security controls to relevant compliance frameworks
* Develop practical risk treatment recommendations

---

## Assessment Methodology

| Phase                  | Deliverable                                      |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Business Understanding | Business model and service analysis              |
| Stakeholder Analysis   | Stakeholder identification and risk exposure     |
| Asset Inventory        | Critical asset identification and classification |
| Threat Analysis        | Enterprise threat landscape assessment           |
| Compliance Mapping     | GDPR, ISO 27001 & SOC 2 alignment                |
| Risk Assessment        | Risk register, scoring methodology & matrix      |
| Executive Reporting    | Top CISO concerns and treatment recommendations  |

## Key Findings

* Customer source code repositories represent GitLab's most critical information asset.
* CI/CD infrastructure introduces significant software supply chain risk.
* Open-source dependency management is a key enterprise risk area.
* Identity and access management remains fundamental to protecting customer assets.
* Governance frameworks such as ISO 27001, GDPR, and SOC 2 support effective cyber risk management.
